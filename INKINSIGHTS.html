GENRES

Non-Fiction

A Short History of Nearly Everything - Bill Bryson

A Short History of Nearly Everything by Bill Bryson provides a captivating and accessible journey through the history of science, unraveling complex concepts and discoveries with humor and clarity, offering a comprehensive overview of the universe, Earth, and the evolution of life.

The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History - Elizabeth Kolbert

The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History by Elizabeth Kolbert explores the ongoing mass extinction event caused by human activities, highlighting the impact of climate change, habitat destruction, and other factors on Earth's biodiversity. The book combines scientific research and compelling storytelling to raise awareness about the urgent need for conservation and environmental stewardship.

Blink: The Power of Thinking Without Thinking - Malcolm Gladwell

Blink explores the concept of rapid, intuitive decision-making and the power of trusting one's instincts, emphasizing the unconscious mind's ability to make accurate judgments in the blink of an eye. Author Malcolm Gladwell delves into the psychology behind snap judgments and how they can be harnessed for better decision-making in various aspects of life.

.Silent Spring - Rachael Carson

Silent Spring by Rachel Carson is a groundbreaking environmental science book that exposes the harmful effects of pesticides, particularly DDT, on the environment and wildlife, leading to a call for the regulation of chemical pesticides and sparking the modern environmental movement.

Freakonomics - Steven D Levitt and Stephen J Dubner

Freakonomics explores unconventional connections between economics and everyday life, challenging traditional assumptions through engaging anecdotes and data-driven analysis. Authors Steven D. Levitt and Stephen J. Dubner reveal surprising insights into diverse topics, demonstrating the power of applying economic thinking to uncover hidden patterns in human behavior.

Between the World and Me - Ta-Nehisi Coates

Between the World and Me is a powerful letter written by Ta-Nehisi Coates to his teenage son, exploring the complexities of being Black in America and addressing issues of systemic racism, identity, and the pursuit of equality. Coates reflects on his own experiences and offers a poignant and thought-provoking examination of the African American struggle for justice and belonging.













Self-Help

The Alchemist - Paulo Coelho

The Alchemist is a novel by Paulo Coelho that follows Santiago, a shepherd boy, on his journey to discover his personal legend and fulfill his dreams. Through a quest for treasure, Santiago learns profound lessons about destiny, spirituality, and the importance of listening to one's heart.

Atomic Habits - James Clear

Atomic Habits by James Clear is a transformative self-help book that emphasizes the power of tiny changes to create remarkable results, guiding readers to build good habits and break bad ones for lasting personal growth and success. Clear provides practical insights and strategies to leverage the compounding effects of small habits, fostering positive behavior change.

Rich Dad Poor Dad - Robert Kiyosaki

Rich Dad Poor Dad is a personal finance classic by Robert Kiyosaki, comparing the financial philosophies of his "poor" biological father with those of his "rich" mentor. The book emphasizes the importance of financial education, investing, and entrepreneurial mindset for achieving wealth and financial success.

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People - Stephen Covey

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen Covey outlines principles for personal and professional effectiveness, emphasizing proactive habits, prioritization, and collaborative synergy to achieve lasting success and fulfillment. Covey's approach promotes a paradigm shift towards character development and aligning actions with core principles for transformative outcomes.

The Power of Now: A Guide to Spiritual Enlightenment - Eckhart Tolle

The Power of Now by Eckhart Tolle is a transformative guide that emphasizes living in the present moment to attain spiritual enlightenment, offering practical insights to break free from the constraints of the mind and find inner peace. Tolle's teachings advocate a profound shift in consciousness to experience a heightened awareness of the present, fostering a path to a more fulfilling and enlightened life.

Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life - Francesc Miralles and Hector Garcia

Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life explores the concept of Ikigai, the intersection of passion, vocation, profession, and mission, providing insights on finding purpose and fulfillment in life based on the practices of the people of Okinawa, known for their longevity and well-being. The book encourages readers to discover their Ikigai to lead a more meaningful and balanced life.













Biography

Steve Jobs - Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs' biography chronicles the life of the visionary co-founder of Apple Inc., highlighting his relentless pursuit of innovation, tumultuous leadership style, and transformative impact on the technology industry. The book delves into his personal and professional journey, portraying Jobs as a complex and influential figure who revolutionized the way we interact with technology.

The Diary of a Young Girl - Anne Frank

The Diary of a Young Girl is the poignant and powerful account of Anne Frank's experiences hiding from the Nazis during World War II, offering a deeply personal perspective on the challenges, hopes, and fears of a young Jewish girl living in hiding. Anne's diary provides a unique and touching insight into the human spirit amidst the atrocities of the Holocaust.

Wings of Fire - A P J Abdul Kalam

Wings of Fire is an autobiography of A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, India's former President and renowned aerospace engineer. The book chronicles his inspiring journey from a humble background to becoming a visionary scientist, highlighting his contributions to India's space and missile programs.n a richly imagined dragon-centric world.

I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings - Maya Angelou

I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings is Maya Angelou's autobiographical account of her childhood struggles, triumphs, and resilience in the face of racism and trauma. The memoir explores themes of identity, self-discovery, and the power of literature as a means of escape and empowerment.

.Becoming: Michelle Obama

Becoming is a memoir by Michelle Obama, offering a compelling narrative of her life, from childhood to her experiences as the First Lady of the United States, providing insight into her personal journey, values, and the challenges of public life. The book explores themes of identity, resilience, and the pursuit of purpose.













Fiction

Pride and Prejudice - Jane Austen

Pride and Prejudice is a classic novel by Jane Austen that explores the complexities of love and social expectations in 19th-century England. The story follows Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates societal pressures, misunderstandings, and her evolving relationship with the proud Mr. Darcy.

The Great Gatsby - F Scott Fitzgerald

The Great Gatsby is a classic novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald that explores the decadence and disillusionment of the American Dream during the Roaring Twenties. It follows the mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby and his unrequited love for the elusive Daisy Buchanan, revealing the tragic consequences of wealth, obsession, and societal expectations.

The Kite Runner - Khaled Hosseini

The Kite Runner is a novel by Khaled Hosseini that follows the tumultuous relationship between Amir, a privileged Afghan boy, and his loyal but socially disadvantaged friend, Hassan. Set against the backdrop of political upheaval in Afghanistan, the story explores themes of guilt, redemption, and the enduring impact of past actions.

1984 - George Orwell

1984 by George Orwell is a dystopian novel depicting a totalitarian society ruled by the Party, led by Big Brother, where individuality is suppressed, and reality is controlled through pervasive surveillance and manipulation of language. The protagonist, Winston Smith, rebels against the oppressive regime but faces dire consequences for challenging the regime's authority.

To Kill A Mocking Bird - Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee is a poignant novel that explores racial injustice in the American South through the eyes of Scout Finch, a young girl witnessing her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defend a black man wrongly accused of raping a white woman during the 1930s. The novel delves into themes of morality, empathy, and the complexities of societal prejudice.

The Lord of the Rings - J R R Tolkien

The Lord of the Rings is a epic fantasy trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien that follows the quest to destroy a powerful ring, as hobbits Frodo and Sam join forces with various races to save Middle-earth from the dark lord Sauron. The story explores themes of friendship, courage, and the struggle between good and evil in a richly detailed and immersive fantasy world.













Romance

Things We Never Got Over - Lucy Score

Things We Never Got Over explores the lingering emotional impact of unresolved experiences, delving into the complexities of moving forward while grappling with unresolved feelings and memories. The narrative navigates the intricacies of healing and the enduring nature of certain emotional scars.

People We Meet on Vacation - Emily Henry

People We Meet on Vacation is a romantic novel by Emily Henry that follows the journey of Poppy and Alex, lifelong friends with complicated feelings, as they embark on a series of annual vacations, exploring the dynamics of their relationship and the possibility of something more.

Book Lovers - Emily Henry

Book Lovers is a heartwarming tale that explores the profound impact of literature on individuals, weaving a narrative that celebrates the transformative power of books in connecting people and shaping their lives. Through diverse characters and rich storytelling, the novel beautifully captures the enchantment and joy found in the world of literature.

It Happened One Summer - Tessa Bailey

"It Happened One Summer" is a contemporary romance novel by Tessa Bailey, featuring a wealthy socialite sent to a small coastal town for a summer of self-discovery, where she unexpectedly falls for a rugged fisherman, leading to a heartwarming and passionate love story.

The Fault in Our Stars - John Green

The Fault in Our Stars is a poignant love story between two teenagers, Hazel Grace Lancaster and Augustus Waters, who bond over their experiences with cancer. Despite the challenges of illness, they embark on a journey of love and self-discovery, navigating the complexities of life, loss, and the pursuit of meaning.

Icebreaker - Hannah Grace

The story follows the developing relationship between hockey player Nathan Hawkins and figure skater Anastasia Allen, two likely enemies who navigate their respective athletic and college careers while falling in love.













Fantasy

A Game of Thrones - George R R Martin

A Game of Thrones is the first book in George R.R. Martin's epic fantasy series, "A Song of Ice and Fire." It follows the power struggles among noble families as they vie for control of the Iron Throne in the fictional continent of Westeros, marked by political intrigue, betrayal, and supernatural elements.

Children of Blood and Bone

Children of Blood and Bone is a fantasy novel by Tomi Adeyemi that follows a young heroine, Zélie Adebola, as she embarks on a quest to restore magic to her oppressed people and confront the oppressive monarchy in a richly imagined West African-inspired world. The story explores themes of power, discrimination, and the struggle for justice.

The Buried Giant - Kazuo Ishigiro

The Buried Giant is a novel by Kazuo Ishiguro that explores a post-Arthurian Britain where a mist of forgetfulness shrouds the land, causing a couple to embark on a journey to find their lost memories and uncover the mysteries of the past. The story delves into themes of love, memory, and the consequences of historical amnesia.

The Last Unicorn - Peter S Beagle

The Last Unicorn is a fantasy novel by Peter S. Beagle, telling the enchanting tale of a unicorn who embarks on a quest to discover why she is the last of her kind, encountering a world filled with magic, mythical creatures, and the struggle between good and evil.

The Fifth Season - N K Jemisin

"The Fifth Season" by N.K. Jemisin is a fantasy novel set in a world plagued by recurring catastrophic seasons. It follows the intertwined stories of three women with unique abilities as they navigate a society marked by oppression, power struggles, and the constant threat of apocalypse.

Assassin's Apprentice - Robin Hobb

Assassin's Apprentice follows the journey of Fitz, a royal bastard with innate magical abilities, as he is trained in the art of assassination while navigating court intrigues and facing the challenges of loyalty and self-discovery in a medieval fantasy world.













Mystery/Thriller

The Silent Patient - Alex Michaelides

The Silent Patient is a psychological thriller novel by Alex Michaelides that follows the mysterious case of Alicia Berenson, a woman who shoots her husband and then stops speaking. The story unfolds through the perspective of a psychotherapist determined to unravel the truth behind her silence.

The Girl On the Train - Paula Hawkins

The Girl on the Train is a psychological thriller novel by Paula Hawkins, revolving around an alcoholic woman who becomes entangled in a missing person investigation, blurring the lines between truth and deception as she uncovers dark secrets. The story explores themes of obsession, betrayal, and the unreliability of memory.

Gone Girl - Gillian Flynn

Gone Girl is a psychological thriller novel by Gillian Flynn that follows the mysterious disappearance of Amy Dunne and the ensuing media frenzy, revealing dark secrets and a twisted marriage. As the search unfolds, the story takes unexpected twists, keeping readers on the edge with its gripping narrative and unpredictable plot twists.

The Turn of the Key - Ruth Ware

The Turn of the Key is a psychological thriller novel by Ruth Ware, revolving around a live-in nanny who becomes entangled in dark secrets and mysterious occurrences while caring for a family in a smart home, leading to a suspenseful and unexpected climax.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo follows journalist Mikael Blomkvist and hacker Lisbeth Salander as they investigate a wealthy family's dark secrets, uncovering a complex web of corruption and violence in Sweden. Together, they unravel a gripping mystery that intertwines financial intrigue and personal vendettas.

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder - Holly Jackson

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder follows a determined high school student who reopens a supposedly solved murder case, uncovering dark secrets and danger in her quest for the truth, blending mystery and suspense.
